##Data Used

**Data** - HR Data with over 22215 rows from the year 2000 to 2020.

**Data Cleaning & Analysis** - MySQL Workbench

**Data Visualization** - PowerBI

## Questions

1. What is the gender breakdown of employees in the company?
2. What is the race/ethnicity breakdown of employees in the company?
3. What is the age distribution of employees in the company?
4. How many employees work at headquarters versus remote locations?
5. What is the average length of employment for employees who have been terminated?
6. How does the gender distribution vary across departments and job titles?
7. What is the distribution of job titles across the company?
8. Which department has the highest turnover rate?
9. What is the distribution of employees across locations by state?
10. How has the company's employee count changed over time based on hire and term dates?
11. What is the tenure distribution for each department?

## Summary of Findings

- The workforce is primarily male, followed closely by female employees, with minimal non-conforming representation.
- White employees form the largest group, with strong representation across multiple racial and ethnic categories.
- Most employees fall between ages 25–54, with the largest group aged 35–44.
- Approximately 75% of employees work at headquarters, while 25% work remotely.
- The average length of employment for terminated employees is approximately 8 years.
- Gender distribution is relatively consistent across departments, with male and female employees represented in all major roles.
- Engineering, Accounting, HR, and Sales have the highest employee counts, while Legal and Auditing have smaller teams.
- The Auditing department has the highest turnover rate, followed by Legal.
- Employees are primarily concentrated across Ohio, Pennsylvania, Illinois, Indiana, and Michigan.
- Employee headcount has shown a steady upward trend from 2000 to 2020.
- Engineering, HR, and Sales demonstrate longer employee tenure compared to higher-turnover departments.

## Limitations

- Some records had negative ages and these were excluded during querying.Ages used were 18 years and above.
- Demographic attributes such as gender and race are self-reported and may be incomplete or inconsistently recorded.
- The dashboard provides descriptive insights only and does not account for external factors such as market conditions, policy changes, or organizational restructuring.
